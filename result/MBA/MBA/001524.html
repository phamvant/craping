2013 年官网 suggestion:
FIRST, an open-ended, introductory question about yourself;
NEXT, a “behavioral” question similar to what you would be asked in a business school or job interview that asks how you handled a certain professional situation;
FINALLY, a “thought” question that offers a statement and asks if you agree or disagree with it, and why. The questions are meant to give us a better sense of who you are, how you act, and how you think.

I would especially emphasize that we do not expect perfection with these questions. We know that they are unrehearsed, extemporaneous, in-the-moment responses. We are more concerned about the thoughtfulness of your responses than the “polish” of the presentation. (That said, please do dress appropriately.)

PART I WHY MBA & WHY NOW

PART II Behavior Questions

Type 1: step out your comfort zone
1. Describe a situation where you identify a problem or opportunity, what did you do?
2.Think about a time when you worked on a highly complex work project. Briefly describe the situation, and tell us the steps you took and whether you were able to complete the project?
3. Tell me an unexpected obstacle you faced, how did you react? Did you overcome it?
4. Describe a situation when your group could take a shortcut, but you did not. Why?
5. Tell us about a challenging work experience and how you handled it?
6. Tell us about a difficult decision and how you handled it.
7. Tell us about a creative solution you designed.
8. What accomplishment are you most proud of?
9. Describe an experience where you need to pay close attention to a task or a project. Tell us the steps you took and whether you were able to complete the task or project.
10. Talk about a time you perform beyond expectation.
11. Describe a personal or professional mistake you held accountable for and how you handled it.
12. Talk about an experience that you had few supervisions, and how you tackled with that situation and the outcome.
13. How did you solved problem from occurring again?
14. How did you solve a complex problem?
15. Talk about an experience that you had few supervisions, and how you tackled with that situation and the outcome？
16. Think about a big problem or project that you had to break down in order to solve or accomplish it. Describe the problem or project and how you handled it.

Type 2: leadership story
1. Think about a time when you had a conflict with a member of your team. Briefly describe the situation and tell us the steps you took to achieve a resolution.
2. A member of team does not share his part. How to deal with this?
3. Describe a situation where a member of your teammate did not perform his/her job. What did you do?
4. Tell us about your leadership style.
5. How will you resolve a conflict with your future classmates at the program?

Type 3: leadership quality
1. What would you say is the biggest challenge facing leaders today? (2014/2015/2016)
2. What do you think is the most important quality for a leader and why?
3. Whose leadership style do you admire the most? and Why?
4. What character you believe every leader should possess? (2014/2015)
5. What is the most important quality to build professional network?
6. What is the characteristic of a great leader? (2014/2015)

Type 4: help others grow
1. Tell us about a time when you help others achieve a goal. What steps have you taken and how you help others achieve a goal.
2. Tell us about how you engaged with a community or an organization.
3. How did you contribute to your company/organization?

12. Social media is a good invention or something that ended true communication.
13. Do you think goods such as alcohol and tobacco should be taxed more? and Why?
14. Whether leader is born or is made, what is your view?
