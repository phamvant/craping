In @ MIT LGO and HBS MBA + Interview Tips

MIT LGO:

  1. Why MIT?
  2. Why LGO?
  3. Tell me a time when someone on the team did not meet your expectation and how did you go about it?
  4. Tell me one time when you are overwhelmed by a situation and how did you handle it?
  5. My interview was on the short side

about 25 minutes. I was really nervous since it was my first interview. I also asked three questions to the interviewer. Sent the thank you letter before hopping on the plane to Jamaica the next day. Spoke with plenty during the interview fest.  Wasn't too sure how I did - I kept on telling people how short my interview was and did not know how to interpret that.

HBS MBA:

  1. Tell me about your work?
  2. What do you do? ( A few follow up questions on products and processes)
  3. Tell me about your team and your office?
  4. What do you suggest you boss continue doing, stop doing and start doing?
  5. Lots of questions on UG experience: Why?
  6. How was it and etc. What is your career vision?

My interview went really well. I was offered to ask questions at the end. I thought that was a positive indicator for sure. I cracked a few jokes ( appropriate ones of course) and felt that I did really well.

I prepared plenty for both interviews. Wrote down answers to possible questions. However, for the MIT one, I spent too much time trying to remember what I wrote down about certain questions during the interview and did not come out too naturally. I also took a day off before the HBS one.

Overall, make sure you know where you want to be and where you came from. Act authentic, professional and show a level of enthusiasm/energy. 

Best luck to everyone else. I know I am blessed. Tough decision for me especially after joining the MIT open house. I am probably going to pick HBS.
