On the eve of its 10th anniversary celebrations, ISB announced scholarships to all Chinese applicants who make it to the ISB’s PGP class of 2013.

All Non-Indian passport holders are eligible to apply for the ISB@10 global scholarships.

The ISB offers a one year full-time residential Post Graduate Programme in Management. The School is currently ranked 13th, by the Financial Times, London. The School located in Hyderabad and Mohali, India is associated with The Kellogg School of Business, Wharton, London Business School Fletcher and MIT, Sloane.

The Post Graduate Programme in Management (PGP) is an incisive programme, ideal for working professionals who want to enhance their careers without a long hiatus from work. The programme is rigorous, challenging and has global professors from Top schools teaching here. Students learn not just from the faculty and their peers, but also from successful leaders and entrepreneurs who frequently visit the campus.

The eligibility criteria for applying to PGP are –

  • Possession of a bachelor's degree in any discipline.
  • A minimum of two years' full-time work experience.
  • A GMAT score. No cut off for GMAT. Scores of past five years are considered valid
  • eTOEFL score is required for students from countries other than India, where English is not the primary language of instruction.

All short-listed applicants are interviewed as a part of the selection process.

About the ISB

Established in 2001, the ISB is a research-driven, independent management institution that grooms future leaders for India and the world. A decade, the School has successfully pioneered several new trends in management education in India, and has established itself as a leading B-school across the world. This is the fourth consecutive year that the School has featured among the Top 20 Business Schools in the Global Top MBA Rankings by the Financial Times, London. The ISB is accredited by the Association to Advance Collegiate Schools of Business (AACSB), and is the first business school in South Asia to be recognized by this premier global body.
